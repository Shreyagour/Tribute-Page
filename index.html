Helen Keller

Philanthropist, author, and eternal optimist

Keep your face to the sunshine and you cannot see the shadows.

A timeline of Keller's life:

  • 1880 - Born in Tuscumbia, Alabama
  • 1882 - Becomes deaf and blind at 19 months old after being afflicted by an unknown illness
  • 1886 - Her parents seek treatments to improve her condition and get referred to the Perkins School for the Blind
  • 1887 - Learns how to spell via her instructor Anne Sullivan who manually spells words into Ms. Keller's hand, then lets her feel the corresponding physical objects
  • 1888 - Attends Perkins and begins studying a variety of subjects
  • 1890 - Learns how to speak and lip read by using her sense of touch with guidance from Sarah Fuller at the Horace Mann School for the Deaf
  • 1900 - Enrolls as a freshman at Radcliffe College
  • 1903 - Publishes her first book, The Story of My Life
  • 1904 - Graduates cum laude with her Bachelor of Arts degree
  • 1908 - Publishes her book The World I Live In
  • 1913 - Makes her first public speaking appearance with Sullivan in Montclair, New Jersey, and recounts how she learned to read, write, and speak
  • 1915 - Co-founds Helen Keller International (HKI), an organization dedicated to researching vision and health issues,
  • 1920 - Helps found the American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU)
  • 1921 - Joins the American Foundation for the Blind (AFB) and tours around the world advocating for individuals with vision loss
  • 1957 - Publishes her book The Open Door
  • 1964 - Earns the Presidential Medal of Freedom awarded by President Lyndon B. Johnson
  • 1968 - Passes away in her sleep at age 87
